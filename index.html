Story

My story begins in Knoxville, Tennessee where I grew up playing soccer, starting in the local rec leagues and ending with 4 competitive Tennessee state championships and 1 TN High School AAA State Championship. Through that journey, I learned what a healthy and selfless team looks like, and that persistence and dedication are vital to all individual success. My experience playing soccer also taught me how to handle conflict when emotions are high and to never take things personally, and I’ve come to learn as I’ve gotten older that all these lessons are valuable wherever I go, as almost nothing truly incredible is accomplished alone - it takes a team.

Before receiving my engineering degrees, my natural curiosity led me to several different roles where I’ve gathered a wide range of experience. One of the most impactful roles to my development was as a universal technician at Terminix where at 21, I was responsible for maintaining a company vehicle, performing all pest and termite contracts in my designated zip code, meeting monthly sales goals, and the customer satisfaction in my zip code. I learned a lot during this time, but the main things were how to educate customers, handle customer satisfaction issues, manage customer emotions, work through pressure, and manage my time while working up to 65 hours a week.

When I began my education at NC State, I maintained my work ethic by self financing my education by working through school at Duke Energy and ImageSoft Inc. and managed to maintain a fairly high GPA while doing this. Additionally, during the final year of my degree, I chose to participate in the Engineering Entrepreneurship Program at NC State, during which I developed a startup called Kast Clothing that made custom fit jeans from 3D body scans. After finishing the program, I was accepted into the NC State Entrepreneurship Initiative's Miller Fellowship Program that provided me a stipend for 6 months to continue developing Kast Clothing.

All my experience led me to the cloud-computing software industry working for the leading hyper-converged infrastructure (HCI) company, Nutanix as a Systems Reliability Engineer 1 (SRE1), fixing business critical solutions for customers ranging from PayPal to the Department of Defense. I am proud to say that I am a part of an exceptional support organization with the highest Net Promotor Score (NPS) and Customer Satisfaction Rating (CSAT) in the industry while working with customers from Paypal to the Department of Defense.

Since joining Nutanix, not only have I continued learning technically at a rapid pace, but I've also continued developing my customer management skills. After about 1 year and 2 months at Nutanix, I was promoted to SRE2, chosen for a new Support Concierge Program for new potentially large accounts, and selected after interviewing to coordinate the SRE1 new hire training.

In addition to my work at Nutanix, in my spare time, I also enjoy helping friends and family by developing and managing websites for their small businesses, testing business ideas and building prototypes of my own, playing and watching soccer, traveling, gardening medicinal and edible plants, and since 2018, scuba diving.

VMware Purple Screen of Death (PSOD) Error Translator

As a Systems Reliability Engineer (SRE) at Nutanix, I often work with customers troubleshooting hardware failures on ESXi clusters, which regularly includes the VMware Purple Screen of Death (PSOD). When hardware fails on an ESXi servers and a PSOD is generated at boot, we have to look at the Machine Check Exception (MCE) that's included in the PSOD to determine what hardware has failed. Specifically, the MCi_STATUS register within the MCE can tell us what we need to replace.

The MCi_STATUS register is displayed next to the “S:” as shown in the example below.

MC: PCPU18 B:13 S:0xfe20004000011166 M:0x7246040086 A:0x38c989b100 5

Before I built this translator, we had to translate the MCi_STATUS register manually by following the steps in the VMware KB referenced below. Working through through the KB takes a fair amount of time and adds the risk for error, so when I learned Nutanix nor VMware had an automated tool to translate the error, I decided to build one.

Reference: VMware KB - Decoding Machine Check Exception (MCE) output after a purple screen error (1005184)

NC STATE FINAL EXAM TIME FINDER (2018)

WHY THIS TOOL WAS MADE:

When you're in high level engineering courses, the professors often don't know the final exam times. Instead, since the information is available publicly (Here), they ask you to look it up yourself. Well the problem with this is that determining your final exam time at NC State is a manual process where students have to use a chart to determine when their exams are, and this of course, introduces the possibilty for human error.

Inevitably, I learned of a friend who missed his exam, because he wrote down the wrong time after a late night of studying. I made this tool so others would hopefully avoid that nightmare.

HOW TO USE:

1. Input your class name (optional - your class will be referenced by the row number without one).
2. Select one of the common exams if you have one. (These are exceptions to the schedule, so be sure to select one if you have one).
3. Enter the day(s) and start time for your class.
4. Press Add Class and repeat steps 1-3 for all your classes.
5. Press Show Me My Final Exam Times, and your results will be shown below.
6. Be sure to add the results to your your calendar and good luck!

SCUBA DIVING

"I work well under pressure."

During my 2015 / 2016 Thailand trip, I snorkeled in the ocean for the first time around the beauitful Phi Phi Islands. The reef was probably 15 or 20 feet below us, so wanting to get a closer look, I learned to dive down while equalizing the pressure in my ear cavities. It was a lot of fun, but I could only stay down for 30 seconds max, because of the higher pressure and physical exertion.

It was this day that the seeds of scuba diving were planted.

About 2 and a half years later, the seeds had taken root, and I had achieved my dream of learning to scuba. I earned my PADI Open Water Diver Certification in the Fall of 2018 with Carolina Dive Center out of Raleigh, North Carolina, and shortly there after, I did my first ocean dives in Playa Del Carmen and Cozumel, Mexico. Since then, I've dove shipwrecks off the coast of North Carolina in the Graveyward of the Atlantic and formed plans to dive across the globe, including the place where it all started - Thailand.
